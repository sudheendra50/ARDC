Establishment of Analog/Digital network using amateur radio communication between Schools and Colleges

SCHEDULE:

Activity Description Timeline
Secure Approval Obtain necessary approvals from school/college administration Month 1
Awareness Sessions Organize awareness sessions and workshops to attract students interested in joining the amateur radio club. Month 1-2
Procure Equipment Purchase necessary equipment like transceivers, antennas, amplifiers, and accessories to meet project objectives. Month 2-3
Establish Station Set up amateur radio stations at each participating school/college, ensuring compliance with legal and regulatory requirements. Month 3-4
Conduct Training Provide training sessions on amateur radio licensing procedures, regulations, operating practices, and basic radio communication principles. Month 3-4
License Acquisition Assist interested students in obtaining their amateur radio licenses through license preparation courses and mentorship. Month 4-6
Hands-on Training Organize practical sessions for students to gain experience operating radio equipment, communicating with other operators, and practicing radio protocols. Month 5-8
Explore Modes Introduce students to various communication modes like voice, Morse code, and digital modes. Month 6-8
Antenna Design Conduct workshops on antenna design and construction to optimize signal transmission and reception. Month 9
Network Testing Establish communication channels between participating schools/colleges and test network functionality. Month 9-10
Emergency Communication Organize simulation exercises to train students on using amateur radio for emergency communication during disasters. Month 10
Public Demonstrations Host public demonstrations and events showcasing the capabilities and benefits of amateur radio communication. Month 11
Project Evaluation and Documentation Evaluate the project's effectiveness in achieving its objectives and identify areas for improvement. Month 12
